Heres a timeline of Nelson Mandelas life.

  • 1918: born in Mvenzo in Tekesi
  • 1934: Attends Clarkebury Boarding Institute in Engcobo
  • 1942: Completes BA through the University of South Africa (UNISA)
  • 1942: Begins to attend African National Congress (ANC) meetings informally
  • 1944: Co-founds the ANC Youth League (ANCYL)
  • 1948: Elected national secretary of the ANCYL
  • 1951: Elected President of the ANCYL
  • 1960: The ANC is banned
  • 1962: Arrested near Howick in KwaZulu-Natal and Sentenced to five years in prison for incitement and leaving the country without a passport
  • 1963: Sent to Robben Island
  • 1963: Appears in court for the first time in what becomes known as the Rivonia Trial, with Walter Sisulu, Denis Goldberg, Govan Mbeki, Ahmed Kathrada, Lionel 'Rusty' Bernstein, Raymond Mhlaba, James Kantor, Elias Motsoaledi and Andrew Mlangeni
  • 1982: Mandela, Sisulu, Raymond Mhlaba and Andrew Mlangeni and later Ahmed Kathrada are sent to Pollsmoor Prison
  • 1988: Moved to Victor Verster Prison in Paarl where he is held for 14 months in a cottage
  • 1990: ANC is unbanned and Nelson Mandela is elected as Deputy President after his relese the same year.
  • 1993: Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize with President FW de Klerk
  • 1994: Votes for the first time in his life and Elected by Parliament as first president of a democratic South Africa
  • 1999: Steps down after one term as President, establishes the Nelson Mandela Foundation
  • 2001: Diagnosed with prostate cancer
  • 2004: Announces that he will be stepping down from public life
  • 2009: Votes for the fourth time in his life; Attends the inauguration of President Jacob Zuma on 9 May and witnesses Zuma's first State of the Nation address; Turns 91
  • 2011: His book Nelson Mandela By Himself: The Authorised Book of Quotations is launched
  • 2013: Admitted to hospital
  • 2013: Passes away at home in Johannesburg
